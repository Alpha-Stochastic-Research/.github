# Roadmap

## Alpha Stochastic Research Roadmap

Alpha Stochastic Research (ASR) is building an open, rigorous, and reproducible research ecosystem in quantitative finance, financial mathematics, stochastic modelling, scientific computing, and artificial intelligence.

This roadmap outlines the main directions of development for ASR projects, repositories, publications, and open-source research infrastructure.

---

## Strategic Vision

ASR aims to become a serious independent research platform focused on:

- Reproducible quantitative finance research
- Open-source scientific computing
- Mathematical and stochastic modelling
- Financial machine learning
- Historical reproduction of foundational papers
- Educational research resources
- Transparent research communication

The long-term objective is to connect theory, computation, and practical financial insight through high-quality open research.

## Current Priority Projects

### 1. ASR Classics Collection

A research collection dedicated to reproducing foundational papers in quantitative finance and financial mathematics.

Initial project:

```text
asr-theory-of-speculation
```

Focus:

- Louis Bachelier’s 1900 Theory of Speculation
- Brownian motion in financial modelling
- Bachelier option pricing formula
- Historical and mathematical interpretation
- Reproducible Python implementation
- Educational documentation

Future possible additions:

- Black-Scholes and Merton option pricing
- Markowitz portfolio theory
- Samuelson’s financial modelling contributions
- Fama’s efficient market hypothesis
- Cox-Ross-Rubinstein binomial model

The short-term roadmap focuses on establishing the ASR foundation.

- Complete default community health files

The long-term roadmap focuses on building a durable research ecosystem.

- Publish open-source quantitative finance tools

## Repository Roadmap Template

Each ASR research repository should define its own roadmap when appropriate.

Suggested structure:

```text
Phase 1 — Foundation
Phase 2 — Implementation
Phase 3 — Validation
Phase 4 — Documentation
Phase 5 — Publication
Phase 6 — Extension
```

Example for `asr-theory-of-speculation`:

```text
Phase 1 — Historical and mathematical review
Phase 2 — Python implementation of Brownian motion
Phase 3 — Bachelier option pricing model
Phase 4 — Numerical simulations and figures
Phase 5 — Documentation and educational notes
Phase 6 — Technical report or article
```
