# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

Alpha Stochastic Research (ASR) is committed to fostering a professional, respectful, inclusive, and intellectually rigorous environment for everyone participating in our open-source research ecosystem.

We are dedicated to providing a harassment-free experience for all contributors, researchers, students, practitioners, and community members, regardless of background, identity, experience level, or perspective.

ASR is built around open science, reproducible research, and constructive scientific dialogue. We expect all participants to help maintain a community based on respect, clarity, collaboration, and scientific integrity.

---

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive research community include:

- Using respectful and professional language
- Welcoming different levels of experience and expertise
- Providing constructive feedback
- Being open to scientific disagreement
- Supporting claims with reasoning, references, or reproducible evidence when appropriate
- Respecting different research backgrounds and perspectives
- Focusing discussions on ideas, methods, assumptions, and evidence
- Acknowledging limitations and uncertainty in technical or scientific discussions
- Helping others learn and contribute

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, intimidation, or personal attacks
- Insulting, dismissive, or discriminatory language
- Trolling, deliberate disruption, or bad-faith argumentation
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing private information without permission
- Plagiarism or misrepresentation of scientific work
- Deliberate manipulation or falsification of results
- Repeatedly ignoring project guidelines or maintainer feedback
- Any conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional research environment

---

## Scientific Integrity

Because ASR focuses on quantitative finance, mathematics, statistics, scientific computing, and artificial intelligence, we place particular importance on scientific integrity.

Participants are expected to:

- Clearly state assumptions
- Distinguish facts, interpretations, and opinions
- Cite relevant sources when discussing research literature
- Avoid overstating empirical or numerical results
- Report limitations and uncertainty honestly
- Respect intellectual property and proper attribution
- Avoid presenting unreproducible claims as established results

Scientific disagreement is welcome when expressed respectfully and supported by clear reasoning.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all ASR community spaces, including:

- GitHub repositories
- Issues
- Pull requests
- Discussions
- Documentation
- Project boards
- Public comments related to ASR projects
- Official ASR communication channels

It also applies when an individual is representing ASR in public spaces.

---

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing standards of acceptable behavior.

Maintainers may take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to behavior that they consider inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Maintainers have the right to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, pull requests, discussions, or other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct.

---

## Enforcement Actions

Depending on the severity and frequency of the behavior, enforcement actions may include:

1. **Correction**  
   A private or public clarification explaining why the behavior was inappropriate.

2. **Warning**  
   A formal warning requesting that the behavior stop.

3. **Temporary Restriction**  
   Temporary limitation of participation in issues, discussions, pull requests, or other community spaces.

4. **Permanent Ban**  
   Permanent removal from ASR community spaces.

5. **Escalation**  
   In cases involving security, legal, or safety concerns, maintainers may escalate the matter to the appropriate platform or authority.

---

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please contact ASR privately.

**Research Contact:**  
research@asr-lab.online

When reporting, please include:

- A description of the incident
- Relevant links, screenshots, or references
- The date and context
- Any additional information that may help us understand the situation

Reports will be handled with discretion and confidentiality whenever possible.
